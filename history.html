Club History

The Foundation

On March 6, 1902, a group of football fans in Madrid Spain decided to start a community club where the best players from the area could play in a team and compete against the other established teams in Spain. This team was called the Madrid Foot Ball Club. Later to be known as Real Madrid, this team already began to make a name out of itself. From 1905-1907, under their first coach, Arthur Johnson( An englishman), the team won three titles in a row in the Spanish League. These marked the first 3 out of 26 league titles won until 2006. However the teams legendary status as a world powerhouse would be set in stone under the leadership of Santiago Bernabeu Yeste. This was the first time the team began to get the best talent from all over the world and not just Spain. By the 1960s the only other group that could compete with popularity in Europe would be The Beatles. A musical band. This was the start of the dynasty.

Team Achievements

In European football, Los Blancos have won a total of 21 tournament trophies. A record 14 Champions League titles, two UEFA cups, and a record 5 UEFA Super Cups. In worldwide competitions, our team has won a total of eight FIFA Club World Cup trophies. We currently have the record for most trophies a team has won in international tournaments. When it comes to the Spanish Cup (Copa Del Rey), our team has currently 19 trophies. After the formation of La Liga, the team has won 35 La Liga titles, with countless other titles when it was just known as the Spanish League.

Club Legends

With a team as prestigious and rich in success such as Real Madrid, there has been plenty of amazing talent throughout the years. However out of the countless talent that there has been, a few have earned their spots as Club Legends. In our modern era, these legends are Zinedine Zidane, Iker Casillas, and the one and only, Cristano Ronaldo. Starting from the goal post, Iker Casillas is regarded to be the greatest goalkeeper our team has ever seen. Playing from 1999-2015, he has made a total of 725 appearances with 266 clean sheets. Going up to the midfield, Zidane’s 5 year run was enough to be of legendary status with a total of 50 goals and 68 assists in his time there. Last but not least, Ronaldo’s impact will forever be enshrined with his 452 goals and 132 assists contribution. These players will always be remembered as Legends of our club.
